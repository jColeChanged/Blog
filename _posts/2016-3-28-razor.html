In the story about the tower of Babel, God cursed the world with many languages. In practice, this means most times I say these things, people don't understand me. So lets start off by defining Occam's Razor to try and avoid misunderstandings. Wikipedia, in the year 2016, had it written that:

Occam's razor […] is a problem-solving principle attributed to William of Ockham (c. 1287–1347), who was an English Franciscan friar and scholastic philosopher and theologian. The principle can be interpreted as stating Among competing hypotheses, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected.

Meanwhile Rationalwiki, also in 2016, put it this way:

Occam's razor is a scientific/philosophical principle which states that one should never make more assumptions than the minimum necessary to explain something; or, if you don't have explicit evidence to believe that something is true, then you should not. The "razor" is used to "shave off" the unnecessary bits of a solution.

It's important to not confuse what is meant by basic. So here are some more quotes to help disambugiate that concept.

Wikipedia states the following three things about the principle:

Occam's razor is not an embargo against the positing of any kind of entity, or a recommendation of the simplest theory come what may.
Another contentious aspect of the razor is that a theory can become more complex in terms of its structure (or syntax), while its ontology (or semantics) becomes simpler, or vice versa.
The telling point that Galileo presented ironically was that if one really wanted to start from a small number of entities, one could always consider the letters of the alphabet as the fundamental entities, since one could construct the whole of human knowledge out of them.

Rationalwiki puts some of these ideas in these ways:

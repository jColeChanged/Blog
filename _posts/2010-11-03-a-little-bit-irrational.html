One of my uncles, Tim, asked me to come run a card game at the local bowling alley. Shortly after accepting his offer, I discovered that playing the card game was a bad idea.

People buy into the game for one dollar and all of their money is put into a pot that goes to the winner of the game. If nobody wins the game the pot carries over to the next week. All you need to do to win is draw the only specially marked card from a deck of five hundred cards. You have five chances.

Breaking things down we find that with five chances to pick the right card from a 500 card deck, we have a 1% chance of winning. So now if we know the pot size we can calculate how much we can expect to win on average.

Average Win = Chance To Win * Pot Size

To get an intuitive sense of why this is so just think for a bit about what the average is. It is the amount won divided by the number of times you played. Now remember that chance to win is a fraction. Even though it looks like you're multiplying when you plug in the numbers your really going to be dividing by 100.

Of course, knowing how much you win isn't very useful unless you factor in how much you had to pay to have a chance at winninga that money. If we put all this together we find that our expected return on each game is our chance to win multiplied by the pot size minus the amount we payed to get into the game.

Average Return = Win Chance * Pot - Buy In

I call this a little bit irrational, because the game is popular.
